Suckers for good coffee

As University of Utah employees who have lived and worked in Salt Lake City for many years, we often yearned for a close, convenient spot to get a quality cup of coffee on the way to work or during a break in Research Park. After many years of wishing and waiting, we took matters into our own hands. We are eager to utilize our collective backgrounds in business, gastronomy, and environmental sustainability to provide a much-needed service to a community in which we are active participants.

About Us

The June Sucker is a fish species native to Utah (specifically Utah Lake and Provo River). It is uniquely ours, but due to ecosystem alteration and mismanagement, June Suckers have become endangered. Now, thanks to targeted research and conservation efforts, populations of this local treasure are beginning to recover.

We are June Sucker Coffee simply because the June Sucker represents our commitment to our local community. We wish to provide customers a responsible, conscious cup of coffee by highlighting locally sourced and ethically produced goods, and by doing our part to protect, conserve, and enhance the vibrancy of our local environment and community.
